Human Resources

About Human Resources:

Human Resources are people who are the workforce of an organization. Human Resources can be administered with Human Resources Management. Human Resources Management is mainly consisted of administering elements of employment and organization's structure. Elements include compliance with legal laws such as employement standards, employee benefits, recruitment and dismissal, unions and more.

Human Resources Career Paths:

HR Consultant

HR Consultants assess the employees' morale or company's culture and structure usually after the merge and acquisition. They consult the management to deploy a new system to achieve the company's goal.

Recruiter

Recruiters are HR professionals specialized in selecting qualified applicants for the job opening. They screen and hire job applicants on behalf of employers. Recruiters also actively seek for new potential employees by attending job fairs and visiting colleges or universities. Other roles include job advertising, interviewing applicants and verifying the information given by applicants.

Training & Development Manager

Training & Development Manager assist new or existing employees improve their skills and careers. The methods can be workshop, orientation, lectures or other types of teaching. It is also an important role for them to design a informative and effective methods for the training.

HR Manager

Human Resources Manager ensures employees receive the proper compensation and benefits, oversee workplace safety, direct the maintenance of employee records, and manage overall employee hiring, evaluation and labor relations. They also develop, implement and oversee training programs or procedures. HR managers must stay up-to-date with policies that govern employee rights, such as equal opportunity employment and sexual harassment.
